Before — a passionate lightsaber community, a growing customer base, and an email program that wasn't scratching the surface of what was possible. After — $501,082.21 attributed to email and SMS. 54.81% of total store revenue. 194% growth.

The situation

NEO Sabers sells to one of the most enthusiastic buyer communities in the market — lightsaber collectors, cosplayers, Star Wars fans who don't just browse, they obsess. The product sells itself to the right person. The problem was the email program wasn't finding the right person, at the right time, with the right message.

Revenue was being left on the table every single day. Flows weren't doing the work they should. Campaigns had no real strategy behind them. And with SMS completely underutilized, an entire revenue channel was sitting dormant.

What we did

We audited the full setup first. Identified every gap, every underperforming flow, every missed automation touchpoint. Then we rebuilt.

The flow infrastructure went up from scratch — welcome, abandoned cart, post-purchase, browse abandonment, win-back. Each one built around the NEO Sabers buyer: someone who researches before they buy, who cares deeply about product quality, and who responds to community and identity-driven messaging. Generic copy doesn't work here. We didn't use it.

Campaigns got a proper cadence and segmentation strategy behind them — new drops, limited editions, product education, community moments. Every send earned its place in the inbox.

Then we activated SMS as a proper revenue channel alongside email — not as an afterthought, but as a coordinated part of the retention system.

The outcome

$501,082.21 in attributed revenue — 54.81% of NEO Sabers' total store revenue driven by email and SMS alone. 194% growth period-over-period, while total store revenue grew 171%.

Flows carried the majority at $321,054.07 — 64.07% of all attributed revenue. Campaigns contributed $180,028.14 — 35.93%. Email drove $479,344.82 at 95.66% of the total. SMS added $21,737.39 — and it's just getting started at 4.34%.

Over half of this brand's total revenue now runs through a channel we built and manage. That's not a marketing tactic. That's retention infrastructure.

Before — average results. Average open rates. No segmentation strategy. Sending to the full list, watching engagement decay. After — 60.4% open rate. 4/4 Excellent ratings across every metric. Industry average is 25–35%.

The situation

An eCommerce brand with a solid product, a healthy list, and results that were average at best. Open rates sat in the mid-twenties — technically not bad, but not indicative of an engaged audience. The problem wasn't the product, and it wasn't the list — it was the strategy. Every campaign went to everyone.

What we did

Full list audit before sending a single campaign. Cleaned the list first — removed unengageable contacts, sunset inactive subscribers, rebuilt segments from the ground up. Only then did we start sending — and when we did, every campaign went to a defined segment, not the full list.

The outcome

60.4% open rate — nearly double the industry average. 4 out of 4 Excellent ratings on Klaviyo's health dashboard, across deliverability, engagement, growth, and compliance. The list didn't just perform better; it became an asset again.

Seven precision sends in 30 days, not carpet-bombing — just strategy that built momentum.
