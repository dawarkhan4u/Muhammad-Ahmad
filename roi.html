Common questions

What DTC brands ask before they start.

Does this work for my product type?
Yes — but the benchmark range shifts depending on what you sell. Consumable and replenishable products (supplements, skincare, food) typically see 25–40% of revenue from email because repeat purchase cycles are short. One-time or infrequent purchase products (furniture, high-ticket items) typically see 15–25%. Gaming, apparel, and collectibles sit in between. The gap in your calculator is calibrated to the industry average — your specific ceiling may be higher or lower depending on purchase frequency.
What if my list is small or cold?
List size matters less than list health and setup. A brand with 5,000 engaged subscribers and a properly built flow infrastructure will outperform a brand with 80,000 cold contacts every time. If your list is cold, the first step is a re-engagement and cleaning process — not blasting more emails. Once deliverability is restored, the flows do the rest. We've seen small lists drive disproportionate revenue because the fundamentals were right.
How quickly will I see results?
Most of our accounts see measurable revenue lift within 30 days of complete setup. Flows go live in the first two weeks — that's when automated revenue starts. Campaigns start generating within days of the first send. The 30-day mark is typically when the data starts compounding: open rates stabilise, segments sharpen, and the system starts working as a whole rather than individual parts.
Why email over paid ads?
Email averages $45 return for every $1 spent in ecommerce. Paid social averages $2–$4 per $1 spent. You don't own your ad account — Meta and Google can change costs, restrict your account, or kill your ROAS overnight. You do own your email list. Every subscriber is a customer or prospect you've already paid to acquire. Email turns that sunk cost into compounding revenue without paying again to reach them.
Do I need to be at a certain revenue level first?
Not a specific number — but there are signals that tell you the time is right. Email and SMS driving less than 30% of your total revenue. CAC on paid channels rising and eating into margin. A Klaviyo account that hasn't been properly set up or updated in months. If any of these describe you, waiting doesn't make the math better. The gap you see in the calculator above compounds every month you leave it.
What's the difference between flows and campaigns?
Flows are automated sequences triggered by a customer's behaviour — abandoned cart, post-purchase, browse abandonment, win-back. They run 24/7 without you touching them. Campaigns are manually sent broadcasts — promotions, new drops, seasonal sends. Flows are set-and-optimise, campaigns are ongoing. Industry data shows flows generate around 37–41% of total email revenue despite being only 2–5% of total sends — because they reach people at exactly the right moment. Both matter. Neither replaces the other.
